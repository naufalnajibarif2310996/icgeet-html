Tourism Recommendations

During ICGEET 2025, take time to explore the beauty of Bandung. An enchanting city nestled in the highlands of West Java. Whether you're planning a short stroll or an extended stay, Bandung offers a captivating mix of cool mountain air, vibrant city life, and rich cultural heritage. Below are handpicked destinations that showcase the city's diverse charm. Additional hidden gems may be suggested by our conference team to enhance your experience.

Kawah Putih (White Crater)

Located about 50 kilometers south of Bandung, Kawah Putih is a breathtaking crater lake formed from a dormant volcano in the Ciwidey region. Its pale turquoise water and white sandy shoreline create a dreamlike atmosphere, surrounded by dense forest and cool mountain breezes. Visitors often find the early morning the most magical time to visit, when the light is soft, the air is crisp, and the area is still quiet, perfect for photography and reflection.

Interestingly, the lake's unique coloration isn't constant; it changes depending on the weather, sunlight, and sulfur content, shifting from milky blue to greenish white throughout the day. Once feared by locals due to its strong sulfuric smell, Kawah Putih remained a hidden gem for centuries until researchers confirmed its safety and opened it to the public in the 1980s.

Situ Patenggang

Just a short drive from Kawah Putih lies Situ Patenggang, a peaceful highland lake surrounded by vast tea plantations and gently sloping hills. This scenic destination invites visitors to enjoy paddle boat rides, sip hot tea by the lakeside, or simply relax in the fresh highland air. The best time to visit is late morning to early afternoon, when the mist has lifted and the surrounding hills are clearly reflected in the calm waters.

The lake is steeped in legend, particularly surrounding a small island and a rock known as Batu Cinta, or the Love Stone. According to local folklore, it marks the reunion of two mythical lovers, and it's said that couples who circle the island together by boat will enjoy lasting affection. The combination of natural serenity and cultural storytelling makes Situ Patenggang a destination with both beauty and heart.

Orchid Forest Cikole

In the cool pine forests of Lembang, Orchid Forest Cikole offers a unique blend of botanical beauty and eco-conscious design. Spanning over 12 hectares, this conservation area is home to thousands of orchid species from around the globe, displayed along scenic trails, canopy bridges, and thematic gardens. Midday is often the best time to explore, as the sunlight highlights the vibrant orchids and filters softly through the tall trees.

Beyond its visual appeal, Orchid Forest serves as a vital conservation site, housing rare orchids that bloom only once a year and require special environmental conditions to thrive. The park also hosts workshops, exhibitions, and cultural events aimed at raising awareness about biodiversity and sustainability, making it an inspiring destination for both education and relaxation.

Tebing Keraton

Tebing Keraton, or "the Royal Cliff," is a stunning viewpoint nestled within a forest reserve in Dago Pakar. The cliff offers panoramic views of Bandung's lush valleys, especially enchanting at sunrise when the landscape is veiled in a soft layer of mist. A short but steep hike leads to the top, where visitors are greeted by sweeping views and the tranquility of untouched forest.

Locals named it the Royal Cliff because the view is said to be fit for royalty. The area has become a favorite among photographers and hikers seeking a peaceful retreat just outside the city. Whether you visit for the sunrise or the quiet afternoon breeze, Tebing Keraton offers a rare chance to see Bandung from above. Calm, green, and vast.

Dusun Bambu Leisure Park

Located in the hills of Lembang, Dusun Bambu is an eco-friendly leisure park designed for families, groups, and nature lovers alike. The site features lakeside restaurants, bamboo gardens, outdoor activities, and unique lodging options such as lakeside villas and treehouse cabins. The best experience can be enjoyed in the afternoon, when the weather is pleasant and the lake reflects the golden light of the setting sun.

The park is inspired by the harmony of traditional Sundanese villages, combining cultural heritage with sustainability. One of its iconic dining spots resembles a giant birdcage overlooking the water, offering not just meals but memorable moments. Every corner of Dusun Bambu encourages visitors to slow down, reconnect with nature, and appreciate the beauty of local life.

Braga Street

Braga Street is Bandung's most iconic boulevard, known for its colonial-era buildings, cozy cafés, and thriving art scene. Once the heart of Dutch high society, this historic street now welcomes a new generation of artists, musicians, and visitors seeking inspiration and leisure. Evening strolls along Braga are especially charming, as the warm lights, live performances, and bustling cafés bring the street to life.

Back in the early 20th century, Braga earned the nickname "Paris of Java" due to its European-style elegance and cultural vibrancy. Today, its legacy lives on through art galleries, mural-covered walls, and vintage architecture. It's a must-visit for anyone wanting to experience Bandung's artistic spirit and colonial past in one walkable stretch.

Asia Afrika Street

Asia Afrika Street is more than just a road. It's a symbol of Bandung's role on the world stage. This historic street was the site of the 1955 Asia-Africa Conference, where leaders from newly independent nations gathered in solidarity. The surrounding architecture, museums, and monuments reflect the city's international legacy, while the street itself remains a hub for cultural festivals and ceremonial events.

A visit during the day allows for a deeper appreciation of the historical landmarks, including the Asia-Africa Conference Museum and statues of prominent world figures. The street is also lined with colonial-era buildings that have been carefully preserved. For those interested in Bandung's diplomatic history, this area offers a meaningful and enriching experience.

Gedung Sate

Gedung Sate, a striking landmark in the center of Bandung, is famed for its elegant architecture and the symbolic skewer-like ornament atop its central tower. Built in 1920, the building combines European and Indonesian design elements and now serves as the seat of the West Java governor. Guided tours offer insight into the building's history and its role in regional governance.

The best time to visit is in the morning or late afternoon, when the gardens are quiet and the view from the rooftop is especially clear. The surrounding area is equally enjoyable, with tree-lined streets, public parks, and nearby cafés. For those curious about Bandung's colonial roots and modern civic life, Gedung Sate is an essential stop.

Mount Tangkuban Parahu

Located about 30 kilometers north of Bandung, Mount Tangkuban Parahu is an active volcano famous for its unique shape, resembling an upside-down boat when viewed from a distance. It's one of the few volcanoes in Indonesia where you can drive all the way to the rim. From the edge, visitors can peer into the massive crater, smell the sulfur rising from the earth, and explore the surrounding landscape of steaming vents and rocky paths.

Tangkuban Parahu is best visited in the morning before the mist rolls in and the crowds arrive. According to Sundanese legend, the volcano was formed from the broken heart of a young man who unknowingly fell in love with his own mother. Today, it remains not just a geological wonder, but also a place steeped in cultural stories and natural drama.

Ciater Hot Springs

Situated on the slopes of Mount Tangkuban Perahu, the Ciater Hot Springs offer a relaxing experience surrounded by tea plantations and mountain air. The naturally heated water is rich in minerals and believed to help relieve fatigue, muscle tension, and skin problems. Visitors can soak in various pools, enjoy spa treatments, or simply unwind amid the greenery.

The best time to visit Ciater is in the late afternoon or early evening, especially after a day of sightseeing. As the temperature drops, the warm water becomes even more soothing. With its serene atmosphere and health benefits, Ciater is a perfect stop for travelers looking to rest and rejuvenate.

Saung Angklung Udjo

Saung Angklung Udjo is a cultural center dedicated to preserving and promoting Sundanese traditional music, particularly the angklung, a bamboo musical instrument recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage. Located in East Bandung, this vibrant venue offers daily performances featuring angklung orchestras, traditional dances, and interactive sessions where visitors can learn to play the instrument themselves.

The experience is especially rewarding in the afternoon when performances are in full swing and the venue buzzes with energy from both local students and international guests. More than just a performance space, Saung Angklung Udjo is a community hub where art, education, and tradition meet, making it a must-visit for those wanting to engage deeply with West Java's cultural roots.

The Great Asia Africa

Located in the Lembang area, The Great Asia Africa is a cultural theme park where visitors can "travel the world" in just one afternoon. The park showcases miniature replicas of architecture, clothing, and cuisine from several countries across Asia and Africa, including Indonesia, Japan, India, Korea, Thailand, and more. Visitors can rent traditional costumes, try regional snacks, and take photos in immersive, well-designed settings.

Late morning to early afternoon is the best time to visit before the area becomes too crowded. This attraction is particularly popular among families and international tourists who enjoy culture-based activities and visual storytelling. It's an excellent choice for those seeking a light-hearted, educational, and Instagram-worthy outing.

Farmhouse Lembang

Farmhouse Lembang combines European-inspired architecture with local charm to create a whimsical tourist experience in the highlands of Bandung. Visitors are greeted by half-timbered houses, flower gardens, costume rentals, and a mini zoo with animals like sheep and rabbits. The fresh mountain air and beautifully maintained surroundings make it a favorite for casual day trips.

Morning visits offer the best lighting for photography and cooler temperatures for walking around. One of its highlights is the "hobbit house," inspired by the Shire in The Lord of the Rings. With its fairytale atmosphere and family-friendly facilities, Farmhouse Lembang is both playful and relaxing.

Floating Market Lembang

Floating Market Lembang is a unique attraction where traditional food is sold from boats floating on a peaceful lake. Visitors exchange coins for local snacks and drinks, which they can enjoy while walking through gardens, playgrounds, and craft areas. The market also features themed villages, boat rides, and cultural shows.

It's best to visit in the early afternoon, when the market is fully open and the surrounding area is lively with local families and tourists. Combining culinary exploration with natural beauty, the Floating Market offers an immersive way to enjoy Bandung's rich flavors and friendly atmosphere.

Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda (Tahura)

Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda, commonly referred to as Tahura, is a vast conservation area located just north of Bandung, stretching from Dago Pakar to Maribaya. Covering over 500 hectares, this urban forest offers a mix of natural landscapes and historical landmarks. Visitors can explore lush walking trails, encounter waterfalls, and visit remnants of Dutch and Japanese caves built during colonial and wartime periods. With its dense canopy and cool air, Tahura provides a refreshing escape right on the edge of the city.

The forest is best explored in the morning when the weather is cool, the trails are quiet, and the sounds of birds and rustling leaves fill the air. In addition to its natural beauty, Tahura serves as a living museum of Bandung's ecological and historical richness. Whether you're hiking, sightseeing, or simply looking to unwind in nature, this protected forest area offers both serenity and insight into West Java's heritage.
